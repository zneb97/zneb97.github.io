The Hand - Sprint I - Animatronic Independent Study

Hand Mechanism

This was probably the simplest simple mechanism I explored, both in terms of understanding the factors that went into the fluidity of the motion and in making. Almost everything was 3D printed and I only added a few holes to the CAD to better attach the hand to the wrist. Like the tentacle, tensioning was by far the most difficult part of the build, especially working in the confined area of the printed wrist. Fortunately, unlike the tentacle, the bungee cords on the back of the fingers could be loosened or tightened independently of the servo controlled fishing line, allowing for less precise tensioning compared to the tentacle.

Working with four of the fingers all going to one servo as opposed to having one for each certainly cut down on the time it took for tensioning but it also limited the visual of the hand, having to always close all its fingers. I did find this could be adjusted somewhat by changing how taunt the bungee cords were but no where near what five servos would have done. While ramping up to my Halloween and final animatronic I will have to be conscious of what functions I will actually want in my animatronic so as not to overcomplicate them and really get to focus on the movements I want to make. Not every hand needs all five fingers actuated. The case could be made that not all tentacles need both pitch and yaw, and certainly not two or more stages.

Like the eye, it was surprising how much a simple cosmetic fix like fitting a glove over the hand (despite being a little small for the hand’s size) did to make it seem more lifelike. In the hand’s case the glove gave it an eerie sense of fullness and weight even when not moving. I’m coming to realize that this definitely helps in not getting bogged down in perfecting every little thing and motion, especially when it comes to tensioning like with the tentacle.
